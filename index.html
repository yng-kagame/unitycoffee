Attract Unity Coffee was born from a deep passion for Rwanda's extraordinary Arabica heritage. Nestled in the heart of Kigali, we source only the finest single-origin beans from Rwanda's renowned coffee-growing highlands — regions celebrated worldwide for their volcanic soil and ideal altitude.

Coffee Education

The World of Arabica

Arabica coffee (Coffea arabica) is the world's most prized and widely consumed coffee species. Rwanda's high-altitude growing regions produce some of the finest Arabica beans on earth, celebrated globally for their complexity and clean cup character.

Origin & History

Arabica coffee originated in the highlands of Ethiopia, where it was discovered and used by native populations centuries ago. It spread through the Arabian Peninsula — giving the species its name — before reaching the world through 15th-century trade routes.

Rwanda's coffee industry transformed dramatically after 1994, evolving from commodity production to specialty-grade beans that now command premium prices on world markets and win international cupping competitions.

  • Grown between 1,400 and 2,200 metres altitude
  • Rwanda's volcanic soil adds unique mineral complexity
  • Bourbon variety predominates across Rwandan farms

Taste & Flavour Profile

Rwandan Arabica is celebrated for its extraordinary cup complexity. The combination of altitude, volcanic soil, and the Bourbon variety creates a distinctive flavour profile that sets it apart from any other African coffee origin.

  • Bright, wine-like acidity with clean sweetness
  • Floral notes: jasmine, rose, and orange blossom
  • Fruit notes: black currant, apricot, and plum
  • Chocolate and caramel undertones in darker roasts
  • Full, velvety body with a long, sweet finish

Health Benefits

Modern research has revealed that moderate coffee consumption carries a range of potential health benefits, making Arabica coffee one of the most studied dietary compounds in the world.

  • Rich in antioxidants that combat oxidative stress
  • May reduce risk of Type 2 diabetes by up to 25%
  • Associated with improved cognitive function and alertness
  • Lower caffeine than Robusta — gentler on digestion
  • Contains essential nutrients: B vitamins, potassium, manganese

Processing Methods

The processing method profoundly affects the final cup. Rwanda predominantly uses the fully-washed process at its network of washing stations, producing clean, bright, transparent flavour profiles that showcase the bean's terroir.

  • Washed process: clean and bright, highlights terroir
  • Natural process: fruity and wine-like sweetness
  • Honey process: balanced, complex and sweet
  • Sun-dried on raised African beds for 2–3 weeks
